# Example: Cup

## Purpose

The cup example tests whether Energy Theory can explain object formation without reducing the object either to physical matter alone or to conscious recognition alone.

## Statement

A cup without form is not a cup.

At cup-scale, the cup is not one simplex.

At physical scale, a cup is a tensor relation of at least two structural simplexes:

1. physical wall simplex, playing boundary / retaining role;
2. space-within simplex, playing interval / containment role.

The physical wall simplex gives form.

The space-within simplex gives containment.

The cup is the tensor relation of physical boundary simplex and space-within simplex.

## Heat transformation

Heat the physical boundary simplex, and the space-within simplex alters.

Transformation chain:

```text
energy-state vector contacts physical boundary simplex
physical boundary simplex modifies its own energy-state
boundary-simplex changes gradient dynamics
gradient dynamics direct vector propagation
space-within simplex alters energy-state
```

## Registration layer

The cup exists physically through boundary and hollow.

A human mind may register cup-whole through a cognitive tensor profile:

```text
archetype simplex
concept simplex
idea simplex
gestalt simplex
```

The gestalt simplex is cognitive registration of integrated cup-whole.

It does not create the physical cup.

## Guardrail

Do not say:

```text
The mind creates the cup.
```

Say:

```text
The physical cup is instantiated by integrated physical/spatial tensor configuration.
The cognitive cup is registered by cognitive tensor configuration.
```
